Unique Interactions in WSP


In a small group, Social Science Research Methods were used to execute research on the interactions between the public and Washington Square Park, NYC. An extensive evaluation through data analysis, graphical data, and density mapping was done to reveal the unique interactions between the public and land use.


As an NYU student, conducting research at a location that holds significance for most students was important. To evaluate how the environment, policy, and land use shape the public's experience at WSP.







Open Street Green Design


Tasked with designing a safe, human-centric design for an open street, I created drawings, diagrams, and data to achieve this. With a small group, research was done on the 34th Avenue Open Street to create pedestrian-centered spaces for the adjacent schools.


I conducted a sun study, utilized AutoCAD & Illustrator to create drawings, and made multiple diagrams showcasing student walkways, connectivity, and water collection.







Policy & the Circular Economy


For my Senior Capstone, I explored the Circular Economy and how it is implemented in different industries and policies.


Through a lens of sustainability, waste, and policy an evaluation of the transition from a linear economy to a circular one has been done before and can continue.
